I would like to invite you to my 30th Birthday Festival!

January 5th, 12.00 - 21.00 in front of Björngårdsvillan, Slottskogen, Gothenburg.

As you might know, ever since I went to my first role playing game at age 13, I've been very engaged in different types of co-creative events. My adventures at Burning Man being the latest example. To me, I see no point in celebrating my personal existance without doing it in collaboration with you guys, the people I love, the ones who make me who I am and make my life so great to live. This day is my celebration of you.

This is what I would like you to do for each other:

- I do expect birthday gifts, only you are not allowed to give anything to me, but only to each other. Instead of asking "What do Gustaf want?", ask yourself "How can I make this party better?", "What can I give to other people?". That is what I want for my birthday.

- The event will be organized in different groups on Facebook. I've started a couple of them (links below), but I also want you to start your own as you come up with ideas for projects and invite others to participate in them.

- Food and drinks will be whatever you bring. Figure out what you want to bring, put together a group of people that will support you (with labor and/or money) and make it happen. How about grouping up and make sure a big barbecue appears? Please try to coordinate and collaborate in fundraising for food in this group: [https://www.facebook.com/groups/g30food/](https://www.facebook.com/groups/g30food/)

- Decorations! Do we need a tent or tipi? Sculptures or climbing structures? Do we need snow lanterns, blinking christmas lights, lasers, spotlights? The group for art and decoration is here: [https://www.facebook.com/groups/g30art/](https://www.facebook.com/groups/g30art/)

- I will put up a sound system and a stage, which will be available from 12.00 - 21.00. However, I will not take part in planning what music or performances will happen. If you want to play, act, sing or scream, here is a Facebook group for that: [https://www.facebook.com/groups/g30music/](https://www.facebook.com/groups/g30music/)

- Dress up! Remember, it will be cold and dark for most of the event, so bring warm clothes and bring lights to attach to your outfit. Teknikmagasinet have several options for battery powered colorful lights that you can use.

- For those of you that travel to Gothenburg and need a place to crash, or for those who would like to host someone, here's a groups for accommodation: [https://www.facebook.com/groups/g30housing/](https://www.facebook.com/groups/g30housing/)

- Yes, there is a backup plan if the weather choose to be against us.

Why is this in english? Well, I have friends traveling in from around Europe, so it is just easier to keep it in one language. I hope that's okay with all my Swedish friends and relatives.

If you have any questions, please direct them to the community. You can of course call me, but I believe that you can do this together. I'll will personally leave these Facebook groups, so I won't know what's coming. Instead I will plan my own surprises for you guys ;)

I love you all,

/ Gustaf

**What does Leave No Trace mean?**

Simple. If you bring it there, you bring it back. Everyone is responsible for keeping their own track and packing it out of Slottskogen.

**Tips & Tricks:**

**Don't let it hit the ground**

It is a lot easier to put your beer cans, food packaging or cigaret butts straight in a container, instead of having to pick them up afterwards. Bring it straight to a trash can or put it in your bag for later. If you smoke/do snus, carry a small container for your leftovers.

**Reduce Your Packaging**

Bringing less in means having less to bring out. Leave unnecessary packaging at home. Food items are a likely target with their many layers of plastic and cardboard, but also consider the toys and camping gear that you’re bringing. Unpacking them before you get home will spare you the hassle of bringing back styrofoam packing and shrinkwrap. Choose cans over bottles, and reusable containers over both.

**Regular Trash Sweeps**

Every now and then we might stop the music and have everyone look around to keep the event tidy throughout the night.

**Be Aware of Very Small Items**

No Trace really does mean *no trace*. Be conscious of spare nails or smaller trash particles that may be dismissed as too small for trash including: hair, matches, cigarette butts, feathers, plastic tie wraps. Remember that food waste such as peanut shells, orange peels and egg shells are also trash. While you're walking around the park, make an effort to pocket all trash, including cigarette butts, and then empty your pockets into a trash bag later.

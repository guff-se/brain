Dear Jane,

My name is Gustaf and I'm the founder of an entrepreneurial scavenger hunt called The Entrepreneurial Chase. Over the course of a day, we send out 8-10 teams on the streets to meet companies and solve realworld problems. I've combined ideas from creative design techniques with game mechanics to create a process that incentivizes out of the box solutions. During one day, the teams get to work with 5 of these problems, in different fields such as marketing, HR, sales or product development.

The purpose of the Entrepreneurial Chaseis to put focus on the people behind the ideas instead of just the ideas (there are way to many idea/business plan competitions in this world). I want to show young people that entrepreneurship is actually something as simple as being able to take your own initiatives and solve problems.

Last spring, we had 120 18 - 25 year-olds competing in 4 cities across Sweden and worked with 20 client companies to solve their problems. The winning team, 3 20-year-olds from Stockholm won a trip to San Francisco as the first prize. My hope is that you would be willing to meet with us during our trip and maybe sit down and share our experiences with gaming as a world-changing tool.

We will be in San Francisco Sep 9 - 17, and our schedule is pretty flexible so far (we're planning other visits to Stanford, Google, IDEO, among others).

We all look forward to meet you,

Yours,

Gustaf Josefsson

More info about the Chase:

The original idea was to create an entrepreneurship competition that focused on the entrepreneur, not just the idea. After my school (IIT) created their Business Plan Team, making entrepreneurship into a purely intellectual exercise, without even a goal to do anything real, I wanted to do something to put focus back on people. While there, I got a sponsorship from Motorola and created the Chicago Entrepreneurial Quest, the precursor to the current Chase, which also ran for two years after I left IIT and expanded to another couple of cities in the Midwest.

After coming home to Gothenburg I decided to start up here. The goal was to inspire more young people to do their own thing and follow their dreams. In May 2009, I managed to create the first Chase, with 50 students from all over Sweden coming to Gothenburg to compete. The event was financed by the local government, but in that process I started to realize that we could actually use this competition to solve real problems, and from there my business model developed. Today I am about 60% financed by clients instead of grants, which makes life a lot more flexible.

The basic idea in my company is that anything can be a game. The recruiting was made by directly qualifying the teams who invited the most people to our facebook group. The PR was done by giving the teams points for getting seen in the media, a trick that generated 50 articles (including the largest national newspapers). I also gave points for getting influential people go on youTube and support individual teams. The list includes some of Swedens foremost business men, a famous news anchor, and Sweden's Minister of Finance.

Every team gets a cell phone with Bambuser, a real-time video broadcasting tool, so every Chase has been broadcasted live online by the participants themselves. The broadcasts have had between 300-1500 viewers each time.

The next step is to expand to more cities, and also to other countries. In October, we will run the first "Entrepreneurial Chase Chase" which basically is a project management and creativity course - made into a game. 10 people (including two of last year's winners) will be a part of this and each of them will then have the freedom to organize their own events using our framework.

That's about it. Thank you for taking your time.

Yours, (again)

Gustaf

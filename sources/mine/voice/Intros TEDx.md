Catarina Moto

Catarina is co-founder of [openMaterials.org](http://openMaterials.org), a collaborative project dedicated to DIY experimentation with smart materials, and altLab, Lisbon’s hackerspace. She is wrapping up her PhD on social impact of open and collaborative practices in technology development, she's a TEDFellow, open hardware advocate. She will set the stage for the discussion about openness and open, collaborative environments, such as the one we are in now, and share here story and thoughts on innovation.

Alicia Gibb

If you ever have Alicia bake your birthday cake, make sure you don't get any of the gears, wheels or computer chips in your mouth. She is a technologist and researcher with a passion for hacking things, including baked goods. Today, the president of open source hardware association and together with Catrarina the co-founder of the open hardware summit. These are her insights about how the current bureaucracy of innovation stifles ideas and how openness drives innovation.

Jannie Jeppsen

There are so many strong opinions about education and how it should be. I, personally, got educated by skipping classes and escaping to the computer lab, away from annoying teachers and pesky class mates. Many talk the talk, but on stage next is someone that walks it too. Jannie Jeppsen is the Principal of Vittra Telefonplan. A 1st through 9th grade school that works practically with redefining education.

Mark Smith

When I met our next speaker, I could tell in a second that he was a univeristy professor. The way he talks, the wild grayish hair and glasses, and this geeky, eccentric passion for his field that can almost only be found in academia. Mark Smith has a vision of breaking out of the university boundaries and create a practical, interdisciplinary work and research space at KTH in Kista. A space for hackers.

Emma Rose Metcalfe

As a designer, our next speaker, have worked on everything from creating experiences at the New York Opera, to help explore molecular science. She has just finished a masters on how to create meaningful experiences. Today, she will tell us about this and also demonstrate her new startup, HowDo, a new social platform to capture and share the things we know about how the world works. Here: Emma Rose Metcalfe

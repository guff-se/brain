My reflections on board composition.

These texts have been shared with the individuals they concern before I posted them in public. All the names I mention here are people who I respect and that I dearly hope to have involved in the future of the Borderland. Any names not mentioned does not mean my lack of support for them.

First of all, I have for the last few years been insisting on making the board smaller, to allow for more efficiency and alignment. I rather see 5 committed voices holding space, than 9 people having a televised debate. If the main purpose of the board is to hold space, they do not have to be representative of certain opinions or populations in the community, they only have to be the best people at holding space.

I would lite to put my wholehearted support behind Daniel Brooks, continuing as chair. Brooks have taken on a difficult role over the last year, trying to oversee a transition towards a less powerful board. I have found him to be brave and committed to uphold his vision of a new organisation, and at the same time deeply care about the emotional wellbeing of every individual partaking in the process. I don’t think that he has not succeed in what he set out, but I think it is unfair of us (and himself) to expect to achieve more in one year. Stepping in to such a leadership position takes a year of learning before you can really start channeling your potential. I believe we should give Brooks a fair chance to share his spark. That requires giving him at least two years in this position.

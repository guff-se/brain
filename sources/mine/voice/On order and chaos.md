Order isn’t a fact of nature, but is rather a point of view. The concept of order stems from the perception of reductablity, that a system is composed of parts that could be reduced into fewer parts.

We know that a word is only a representation of what actually is. Yet if it is good enough for our purposes, we will accept that the word “apple” does not actually reflect the color, taste or even eatability of what is represented.

This is the compromise we live with every day.

Meaning = order

Chaos vs order

Acceptance vs strife

The monk vs. the warrior

I like Pantheos, the god that is all. To me that is the only God that exists, The Divine, as Alex words it. I don't care much for Athos, as I believe that the philosophical "Void" as described in Zen (as opposed to just "nothing", as described by Lawrence Krauss) is a fairly uninteresting philosophical dead end.

I enjoy the worship of entropy, a scientifically beautiful, simplistic concept that not only has the pontential to explain the entire universe, and also predict its inevitable end. I shy to call it Entheos, as I feel it is distinct from just "difference" (or "enthusiasm", as Alex have previously associated Entheos with). To me entropy is a mechanical force that drives the eternal battle between order and chaos, between meaning and nonsense, between acceptance and strife. Speaking of that, Ilaneris, God of the Illusive Order and Chaos, the God of Acceptance are two Gods I rather worship than Atheos.

However, these are just my personal projections, and in every way equally void of external meaning as any other aspect of God / The Divine / The Universe / Pantheos / "the thing that is". We _can_ believe in them, as Alex mentions, but only knowing that any so called "aspect" of "it" will inevitably be over-simplified constructs, shallow caricatures 

* There is only The One. The One is above all others. It is God, it is the Universe, it is "what is". The face of the One is studied through the practice of science and introspection. The One has two primary aspects, the Illusion of Order and the Acceptance of Chaos.

* The Illusion of Order represents "meaning", the idea that something could be represented by something smaller than what is. Order are concepts, words and relationships. It is all we know and all we ever can know, since our consciousness constructs it and is constructed by it. It is the act of creating. It is represented by the dark, the cold, the moon.

* There is the Acceptance of Chaos. Since The One, not in the whole, nor in any part can be represented by something other than itself. Any word, any concept, any thought, any sense of order are just illusions. Therefore the other aspect of The One is the Acceptance of Chaos, the dissolved self, the ecstasy. It is all there is, but something that never can be reached by our consciousness. It is the act of accepting. It is represented by the light, fire, the sun.

There. I've invented a believable God. Now, obviously, I haven't spent that much time doing it, so a lot is still to be done. But that's not the point.

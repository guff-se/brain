I was the person being dragged to the city, beaten, and sacrificed on Saturday night. I would like to provide some background to why I did it and what it meant to me.

On Friday night, I was approached buy two friends of mine about “The Summoning of the Black Unicorn”, a ritual by a dark group that I had previously seen in procession around The Borderland. They told me that they would like to end the week by making a sacrifice of a “white unicorn”, someone who could represent the “light”. They asked me if I would like to do it.

For those of you who don’t know me, I’ve been involved in The Borderland ever since the beginning. In 2013, I stepped up as the chairman, and the core organizer of The Borderland, a role I had for 3 years. Over the 5 years that has passed, I’ve had pretty much every job that one could have. I used to build the power grid on my own, I’ve set up the web site, the ticket system, the wiki. I started the Clown Police and the predecessor to The Port. I’ve built big art, organized sound camps, and hosted workshops and ceremonies. I’ve been the first person and last person on site. I was the one who stood on stage to deliver the message to our community that we had lost one of our participants to drowning, shortly after delivering the same message over phone to his best friends at home.

Over the last three years, I’ve tried to step down and redefine my leadership. And for the first time this year, I truly felt that I was not responsible. All the burden and sense duty that I had carried over the years (actual and imagined), had lifted from my shoulders. With that came both an immense happiness, a pride and sense of relief, but also a grief, a sudden realization of how much pain I have caused myself through my work and my passion. All the times I have cried, all the conflicts I have gotten myself into, the exhaustion, the physical pain of overdoing myself. The toll this self-inflicted sense of duty has taken on me and on my closest relationships. And maybe most of all: how lonely I have made myself feel through may of these moments.

When I joined the The Summoning of the Black Unicorn, I had to make the story mean something to me. What kind of “light” do I represent? Why am I being dragged through he streets in humiliation? Why am I not fighting back? Where am I heading?

As I stepped in to the character, I decided to let this march represent the burning of my self-righteousness. What was dragged through the city was my own clinging to the belief that my way is the right way, the good way, always the best way for the community. I allowed myself to fully go in to my sense of being all-knowing, all-seeing Goodness, to step in to my inner Jesus - and have that be defiled.

I allowed myself to cry over having lost, of finally having the dark powers of other people’s will take over. Of having lost control over the beast that is The Borderland. Of not having it all my way. To despair over the uncertainty of not being in control. To be punished for my self-obsession, to be humiliated for my sense of pride, to be sacrificed for letting my ego hurt myself and others.

You know, the average kind of method acting. If you want to play the part, you need to fucking BE the part.

To me, this became one of the most memorable experiences of my years at The Borderland. I felt so much trust for the sweet, caring and humble people of who hosted the ritual, in a way that allowed me to go fully in to my feelings of helplessness. As we walked through the city and I felt the eyes of others on me, I had a strong sense of relief, of having been thrown off the pedestal. To have gotten out of the character that I love to play, and that so many of you love me playing: the funny, charming, intelligent and charismatic “Gustaf”. I allowed myself to be weak, and feel worthless in front of everyone.

Thank you for those who took me on this journey. Thank you for all of those who watched, in amusement, or in despair.

So, was participating in this ritual reckless and irresponsible? Should we have “asked for consent” before doing it (as if that would have even been possible?). Maybe this was just even more of my personal self-indulgent, ego-centered self-expression, without regards to others or what the “community” actually wants?

I understand some people got pushed out of their comfort zone by watching this experience, inducing one of my closest friends and house mates. For those of you that felt negatively impacted by this performance: I hear you, I see you, I love you.

When we all seek what is needed for ourselves, there will be conflict of interest. In this case, my self-expression treaded on your sense of inclusion. These are tensions that we have, and the will keep having, and that we should be having. If anyone wants to talk about their experience in relation to this ritual, please reach out to me privately and I would love to hear you. The Borderland is not a safe space, but neither should it be a danger zone. It is the land on that border, where we sometimes feel discomfort, where our sense of self and sense of reality gets questioned, but where we are held when the shadows come to close. We do not all seek the same things, but we are there together.

I had an absolutely wonderful Borderland this year. I brought my parents and my uncle for the first time, which added more depth to the experience. I was fortunate to be a part of the best camp I’ve ever been in, the Shanzhai Fortune Tellers. I helped out where I felt my help was needed. And I had one of the best Borderland nights in a long time on Friday night.

Life is a fucking blast! And I have this community to thank for so much of that. Thank you for sticking with me, or fighting against me! Thank you for loving me, criticizing me, supporting and challenging me. I am so proud of you, and I am so proud of myself!

I might have been sacrificed to darkness, but you will not get rid of me that easily. <3

Urgent reason for celebration!

The Borderland will be moving Boesdal Kalkbrud in Denmark! As the location is now set, we will be opening up for another 200 co-creative memberships, to be released at 20.00 on Sunday March 29.

Boesdal is a 15 hectar limestone quarry, just on the ocean, a 1,5 hour drive south of Copenhagen, Denmark. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, that contains a varied landscape of meadows, rocky beaches, gravel racing roads and steep dunes of limestone sand. Inside the quarry, there’s a conical storage building, 22 m high and 60 m diameter, an abandoned factory building as well as a modern camp site with a couple of wooden windbreaks. The quarry is owned by the local government and cared for by a local non-profit culture organisation that are leasing it to us.

Since we have set our location, we are now ready to release another 200 co-creative membership, bringing the total co-creator count up to 500 people. These will be release first-come-first-serve, just as the previous 300. Registration will open up at 20.00 on Sunday March 29 on this page: http://borderland2015.eventbrite.com.

Once again, we set out for exploration, to find out what exists on that thin border between reality and dreams. Once again, The Borderland will rise from the collective consciousness of it's citizens.

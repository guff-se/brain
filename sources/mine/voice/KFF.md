Ok.. first up, keynote...

I’ve done over 500 professional talks over the last 10 years and was nominated speaker of the year in Sweden last year. Getting to play with both my experience as a Burner and as a leadership developer would _kick_ ass.

I will be producing a ”co-creative leadership development program” for co-creative event producers throughout this summer and will have the first kickoff retreat on the weekend just before KFF.

The talk I’d _really_ like to give is:

"Scaling personal transformation through participatory culture.” (45-60 min)

Lessons from The Borderland and Burning Man on how to build self-organizing communities that foster psychological development, and how this might be applied to build a better society.

Alternatively, (or additionally), I could do workshops.

Number one suggestion

”Getting off the pedestal” (60 min)

How to develop status fluency, a practical workshop on power and hierarchy in self-organization.

This is a workshop I developed from experiences at The Borderland, and have used to train executives. The idea is to become more intimately connected with how you play up and play down status in relationships.

I could also do:

Curiosity (30-60 min)

A workshop designed to deepen people’s courage to ask questions that they truly care about. I’ve done this in 50+ settings over the last few year. The workshop is described (in Swedish) here: [http://nyfikenhetsleken.se](http://nyfikenhetsleken.se)

Finally, I could bring some of my wife’s art. Posting 3 alternatives, of varying complexity and size. These are optical fibre textile sculptures, and require dark(ish) rooms to look really nice.

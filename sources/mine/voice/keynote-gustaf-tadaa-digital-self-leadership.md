A condensed competition talk. Gustaf opens with the overused exponential curve, then collapses the implication: in the transformation age we spend more time changing what we do than doing it, and no manager can write meaningful job descriptions anymore — so every employee's primary job is to figure out their own role in the network. The Library of Alexandria in everyone's pocket means leadership skills (knowing your strengths, traumas, goals) are now basic life skills, not executive training. He pivots to Cambridge Analytica: Facebook profiles predicted personality better than your partner. "Know thyself" is harder when self is increasingly digital. Closes by arguing personal and digital development must be treated as one.

## Key claims

- Every employee's primary job description is now to figure out their own job description.
- Leadership skills (self-knowledge, goals, empathy, creativity) are basic skills everyone needs to function in society today.
- Cambridge Analytica's research showed Facebook profiles could predict personality better than friends, family, partners.
- Most people have surrendered the power to put thoughts into their own brains by not curating their notifications.
- Personal development cannot be treated as separate from digital development — you must integrate "the you you found on the mountain with the you in your pocket."
- Technology is the only thing that ever made us human.

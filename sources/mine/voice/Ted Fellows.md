If a friend were to describe your accomplishments in up to three sentences, what would he or she say? *

Gustaf dares to try on any task, any subject, even if he's got no idea what he's doing. Wherever he goes he brings a spark of creativity and dedication. He's brought hope and inspiration to the young people he meets and managed to create a brewing network of creativity in his tracks.

What other achievements (not only academic) would you like to share? *

I've had 7 majors (cognitive science, rhetoric, chemistry, law, biomedical engineering, business and psychology) in 4,5 years (with 6 years worth of credit) in 3 schools, 3 different citys on two continents, finally ending up with a B.Sc in Psychology.

I started my first business when I was 18. From 2003-2008 then I've been coaching young entrepreneurs one week every summer at Emax ([http://www.emaxnordic.com/](http://www.emaxnordic.com/)), I was the president of the IIT Entrepreneurship Academy at Illinois Institute of Technology and also worked at the entrepreneurship program there. Since 2008, I run my own company, "The Entrepreneurial Chase" (http://entrepreneurialchase.com), working to create more young entrepreneurs in Sweden. I was in Dec 2009 elected president of Young Entrepreneurs of Sweden ([www.yeos.se](http://www.yeos.se)), a network consisting och 250 of Sweden's best entrepreneurs under 25.

Beyond your work and studies, what are you passionate about? (hobbies, causes, activities, issues…) *

Creativity is my favorite drug and I try to spend as much time as possible creating. Be it new companies, products, projects, art, music, new words or new concepts; creativity is my wheel of life. I have a huge passion for board games, and also work with developing live role playing games. (Side note: Live role playing in Sweden is more geared towards interactive theater, not running around in the woods dressed up like Gandalf).

My latest side project is to bring Swedish culture to the participatory art experiment Burning Man. After visiting for the first time myself in 2009, I came home and raised money to put a container in the back yard of one of Gothenburgs art museums. A group of 20-30 known and unknown creators will be running workshops there all throughout the spring and summer, with the mission to "interpret swedish culture". In July, we will pack everything up and ship it over to Nevada to create our own Swedish Embassy in Black Rock City.

Share an example of something you have been a part of (including created, led, or joined) that you consider unique, even if no one else does. Why do you think it is mould breaking? (Limit 2250 characters - not words!) *

I am the founder of the Entrepreneurial Chase, a company that let's young entrepreneurs (aged 18-25) solve problems for companies.

Despite the fact that young people have more to contribute to society than ever before in human history, we have the highest youth unemployment rate that Sweden ever had (30% for ages 18-30). I believe this is caused by the generational gap caused by the shift from a knowledge-based society to a learning-based one. People born in the 80's just don't fit in to the old, seniority focused hierarchies, and are therefore left stranded. In Sweden, many carry a mental barrier making entrepreneurship inaccessable. Despite more support networks and easier regulation than in, for example, US, this barrier prevents many young people from starting their own business. The purpose of the Entrepreneurial Chase is to show that young people really can contribute to corporations, and to show that "doing your own thing" is easy. You don't have to invent Google to start your own business, you just have to find a problem in your surrounding that you can contribute to solving.

The Entrepreneurial Chase is form of scavenger hunt, where 10 teams of young entrepreneurial minds travel around a city, visiting 5 different companies over the course of a day. At every stop, they help the companie with creative business consulting, adressing topics from marketing and product development to sales management and recruitment. By formatting and packaging the benefits of being young and make it sellable to older, more square corporations, I'm trying to bridge the gap between old and young, between knowlede and creativity. I believe that we can turn the gretest youth unemployment rate to the greatest youth business startup rate. To me, this is "social entrepreneurship" - a way of making money by doing something good for society.

I started the company in 2009 and ran the first "chase" in May, involving 5 customers and 50 young entrepreneurs. Today, I employ 3 people (all former competitors)  and this spring I run 5 competitions in 4 cities, involving 120 young entrepreneurs from all over the country and work with 20+ paying customers (companies).

I think the obvious question would be "Why do you want to become a TED Fellow?"

My passion in life is to explore what's next. I can sit for hours with friends, trying to predict the future, how society will work and what businesses to start. Since a few years back, one of the cornerstones of the discussions that's been going on in my social circle emanates from TED.com. I visit the site several times a week and have seen over a hundred videos. When TEDxGotenburg started up in my hometown wanted to be one of the speakers, but had to settle with participating. Working with ideas is my life and I believe that many of my own ideas are "worth spreading".

Since last summer, I've been increasingly convinced that I have to do more public speaking. While working with the Entrepreneurial Chase, i've also tried to develop my brand as a speaker and sell my talks to more venues. Mostly to develop my presentation and my speaking skills. My main topic is the entrepreneurial revolution or "the long tail of entrepreneurship", how increased accessibility to information, as well as tools of production and distribution has leveled the playing field for entrepreneurs. I believe the 21 century will be the century of the entrepreneur, where the network based economy will bring balance, equality and social change to the world.

One of my goals in life, that i'm currently working on is to speak at TED. In my kitchen at home, as well as by my office desk, i've printed signs saying "TED 2011". I don't know if the number is right, but I know I will get there eventually.

Can you share a memorable anecdote from your life that will give us a further sense of what makes you tick?

In 2002, 19 years old and just graduated from high school, I was a part of the collaborative art festival "Futuredrome". After participating I wanted to contribute to the community in the best way i knew how, so I programmed a social network. Over a few months, while living in my parents basement, I developed a social platform where the 1500 participants of Futuredrome could communicate. I uploaded the 1000's of pictures that had been taken by photographers, as well as the over 100 hours of video clips that had been created as a part of the Futuredrome movie project. I made it so that all the participants could tag and comment all the material, so that we could all help out in finding pictures of ourselves and each other. I later developed this in to a collaborative project management tool, designed to be used at the follow-up event the year after. Unfortunately the event was cancelled due to financial problems and I ended up spending an entire year in my parents basement developing a precursor to facebook and youTube - all for nothing.

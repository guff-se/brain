This is what a newfound friend recently told me. She’s in her late twenties, well educated, has a good job. She is both intelligent and emotionally mature, having put a lot of time into personal development, meditation and self leadership. Butt this was a moment that completely changed her.

She told me that over time, she had collected deep knowledge and insights, but it wasn’t until this moment she was fully able to understand and practice what she had learned. This relatively short moment in time provided a transformation from one stage in life to another.

These transformational experiences seem to be one of the central components of personal development. I’m sure many of you have had one or more in your life. Those moments where an insight just dropped. Where something, which has already inside you, just fell in place.

Trying to create these transformational experience is something many retreats, leadership courses and events design for. But this was different. The story played out at a small retreat of 200 people in a farm in the middle of Sweden. But the experience wasn’t designed, it was allowed to emerge, it was co-created.

This event was a part of a network of hundreds of events world wide, with half a dozen in Sweden just in the last year. It was an event built on the principles and ideas of Burning Man.

Michael Michaels, one of Burning Man’s original founders (and known as “Danger Ranger” at the event), explains it this way: “At Burning Man, we’ve found a way to break out of the box that confines us. What we do, literally, is take people’s reality and break it apart. Burning Man is a transformation engine—it has hardware and it has software, you can adjust it and tweak it. And we’ve done that. We take people out to this vast dry place, nowhere, very harsh conditions. It strips away their luggage, the things they’ve brought with them, of who they thought they were. And it puts them in a community setting where they have to connect with each other, in a place where anything is possible. In doing so, it breaks their old reality and helps them realize they can create their own.”

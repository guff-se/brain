Once I met a girl. She was curious and creative, caring and bright. I was young and afraid, I dreamed but didn't dare. 

We sat on a rock, overviewing the sea, looking into the sunset as it changed to sunrise. We drank wine, we laughed, we created futures beyond belief. We were both young and afraid, we dreamed but we didn't dare.

Destiny took us apart, and for years I travelled the world searching for a home. I found a place remote, far away from the eternal sunshine. I found others who dreamed, but who also knew how to dare. I grew, I changed. I took the step.

I was approached by a friend who also wanted to find this home, but who didn't know how to travel. We needed a vessel to take us on the journey.

We saw a ship. A vague vision at first, a vessel that was clearly a figment of imagination. But, oh, what a vessel! A great Viking Ship! With full sails, burning as it sailed towards the horizon. If we could create it, if we could build it, it would take us home.

But we could not travel alone. We needed more brave warriors to construct our ship. Suddenly she was there.

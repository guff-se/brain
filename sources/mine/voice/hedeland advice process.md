I believe that it is likely that The Borderland will split into two festivals in 2018. If this advice process ends up with a decision to stay in Hedeland, there is a contingency of people who would like to move on, who are more eager to push the boundaries instead of conforming to the 

If we end up moving, and end up moving out of Denmark, I think it is likely that something new will pop up… maybe in Hedeland.

The Borderland has, up until now, been very heavy on the Swedes. In 2018 there were about 45% swedes and 30% danes (last year 45%/19%). There was about as many from Stockholm as from all of Denmark. These proportions have been about the same (1:2 - 2:3) since 2012. The board this year was Stockholm based, and leadership in general has historically been very Stockholm heavy. Working Class Heroes, Clown Police and Sanctuary have all been directed from Stockholm.

Now, I say this with utmost humility. I’m not trying to credit one group over the other, and I’m not saying that this is the way it should be. Quite the opposite, I think that it might be the case that the Stockholm mob have been a roadblock for leadership development within the Danish community. As humans do, we have tended to look to our own and prioritized convenience over inclusion.

Maybe it isn’t so much about IF we should organize The Borderland at Hedeland next year, but WHO will be organizing The Borderland next year.

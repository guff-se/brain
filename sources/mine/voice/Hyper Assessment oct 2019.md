**Isabella Hallin**

Thank you for your presentation Isabella. A general feedback is that you listed a lot of things that you did in general terms (I did worshop x, used tool y) and that I often were lacking more concrete examples of what you actually did and learned. You gave me the chance to get some more evidence in the end, but it was so many things that a had been expressed in these general terms that it was hard for me to navigate. My advice would be to maybe chose to talk about fewer things but be more specific about those: what you did, why you did it, what you learned.

I have given you a no pass on two of the criteria. On S1, according to your own definition “business” means "Increase a company’s currency (money, users, awareness)”. However, I was missing this from your presentation. You covered the “develop” and “future proofing” parts, but didn't manage to relate this to any type of “currency”. How are you creating business? You mentioned you had realized that the subscription industry is huge, but to me that is not enough to show that you actually have the skill of developing a digital business concept.

The second is S10, where I found you to go back to things I connect more to client assessment and design, missing the focus both on “develop” and “solution". I’m missing building actual solutions and iterating on them. Your answers are probably in there somewhere, but I found it really hard to assess this goal, because you mentioned so many things that more belong in S4 or S7. My suggestion is that you make it very clear to yourself what the difference between “design” and “develop” is.

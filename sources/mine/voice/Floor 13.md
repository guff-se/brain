Some cultures are more developed than others.

I believe that to be a useful statement. If all cultures are equal, there can be no such thing as cultural evolution or cultural progress.  To say that some are more "developed" is not to say that they are "better", in the same way that I do not claim to be a "better human" than my 1,5-year-old daughter. I am, however more developed than her.

So how do we determine which cultures are more developed than others. This is a complex task, riddled with biases and self-serving interests. First of all, we have to acknowledge that one culture can be more developed in one aspect and less developed in another. Second, we have to be very careful not to confuse "different" with "less developed", especially in a country with high self-image, such as Sweden. This said, we have to dare to make claims about cultural development

We live on floor 13 in our building. In some cultures, the number 13 is removed from the floor numbering of apartment buildings, so that the floors are named 11, 12, 14, 15. I'm going to make the claim that cultures that do have a floor 13 are more developed than those who don't.

What other statements about cultural developments do you find useful?

And, please, be respectful.

Cultures that honor elections are more developed.

Cultures that provide free education all citizens are more developed.

Cultures that consider women and men to be equal in front of the law are more developed.

Cultures that don't exercise control over who you get to love with are more developed.

Cultures that welcome strangers are more developed.

Cultures that accept a larger diversity of self-expression are more developed.

Cultures that accept more forms of emotional expression are more developed.

Cultures  with less physical violence are more developed.

Cultures with less threat of violence are more developed.

Cultures with less shame are more developed.

And finally: Cultures that play less psytrance are more developed.

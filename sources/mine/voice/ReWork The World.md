- What problem is addressed?
I believe that the largest gap between thought and action, between idea and idea realization is the false belief that "doing" is more difficult than it actually is. While technology has leveled the playing field when it comes to production, marketing and distribution, making entrepreneurship more accessible to everyone, there is still a difference in perception, depending on culture and social economic status. The goal of The Entrepreneurial Chase is to increase the mental accessibility of entrepreneurship for everyone and show that realizing your ideas is something you can "just do".

- What is the solution?

The Entrepreneurial Chase is a scavenger hunt, focused on showing young people that entrepreneurship can be easy, and that all young people can find their own ways of adding value to companies, projects ideas or processes. By embracing entrepreneurship-by-play the mental distance between idea and realization becomes shorter.

- How is it delivered?
During the course of one day, young entrepreneurs are sent out to help corporations with creative problem solving. The assignments cover a range of essential entrepreneurial  skills, such as marketing, product development, risk management and human resources. The participants get no prior information on where to go or what do, as they explore the city step by step. Each assignment is about and hour long, and consists of a problem, coupled with a method to create a deliverable within the timeframe. Some happen inside corporate offices, others happen online or out on the streets. Every chase has five assignments, from which points are added up for the final score. As this isn't an idea competition, but a assessment of entrepreneurial talent, the competing entrepreneurs bring no ideas with them. All that counts is what happens in the competition.

- What is the track record of the organization?

50 young entrepreneurs competed in Gothenburg, in May 2009. During the spring of 2010, the organization has expanded to 4 regional competitions around Sweden, and a final in Gothenburg on May 7, with 120 young entrepreneurs (age 18-25) and 40 partnering companies and organizations. The event is also followed by thousands of people through real time web-tv, facebook and twitter. The PR-competition that was a part of the 2009 chase generated 38 articles in both national media and local newspapers all over Sweden.

- What is the ambition? (concrete indicators preferred)

The goal is to expand to at least 8 cities in 2010-11, and to also expand to Norway, Denmark and Finland within 2010 and hopefully inspiring thousands of young entrepreneurs along the way. As one of last years particiapts put it: "The Entrepreneurial Chase was the greatest thing I have ever experienced. Now I understand what it means to be an entrepreneur." (Naim Murseli, 19 yrs, runs 2 businesses)

- How many jobs could it create over what timeline?

Unless your project is already done, extremely uncreative (such as building a road), or you're a psychic, I believe this number is impossible to assess. It would be wrong of me to claim that I'm able to predict the number of jobs created in a new project, primarily focused on attitude change. That said, my friends at Hogwarts believe we can create over 10 million jobs world wide in a 100 year timeline.

Using Slack for NWTA communication

Slack is a digital tool for team communication, which MKPNordic is experimenting with. It makes it easy for brothers to start group conversations, so that questions to be answered and problems be solved without the involvement of the core workgroup. The hope is to use this to make it easier for staff members to take initiative, coordinate tasks and seek advice from other brothers.

You will receive an invite to the NWTA March 2020 Slack team shortly, and you may join the conversation there. All essential information will be sent out through email, so participation is not required. We invite you to try it out.

---

Welcome to Slack!

For those of you who are new, this is a digital communication tool with the purpose of supporting the Nordic MKP community in becoming better at sharing knowledge, offering each other support and make coordination of NWTA’s more efficient.

Conversations here happens in #channels, rather than in strict groups (such as a normal email thread). Anyone can create a channel and anyone can list channels and join them. There are already some channels up for you, such as #homecomings, #mentoring, #medical and #ebi (even better if), which you may join if you are interested or have questions regarding those topics. There are also channels that some of you have been automatically added in if you belong to a certain group, such as #mos for our Men of Service and #1st2nd for 1st and 2nd time staff.

The hope is that questions can be asked and support can be offered between brothers without the need for central coordination, or for the person asking to know specifically who to ask. This while keeping communication transparent and in one place, so that it does not get lost in forgotten email threads.

Slack is still an experiment for the community, but we hope you give it a try. It thrives on the “network effect”, so the more people that actively use it, the more efficient it will make us.

Finally, I’d like to suggest that you actively manage your notifications (both here and in life). If you click the “bell” in the top left and open “your notification preferences”, you will have the ability to choose how and when you want to be notified about updates on Slack.

If you have any questions, don’t hesitate to ask!

(But don’t ask me! Ask here in the channel. That’s the entire point!).

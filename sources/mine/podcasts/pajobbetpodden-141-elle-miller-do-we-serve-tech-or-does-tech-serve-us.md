English-language conversation with Elle Miller, leader in Slack's services team. The team works at the intersection between digital tool and organizational behavior. Conway's Law: software systems mirror the organizations they were designed for. Slack works best in flexible, lateral cultures and struggles in top-down memo-driven ones.

## Key claims

- Slack's services team applies Conway's Law: implement to mirror existing organizational structure.
- Slack is more than chat — a platform for integrations and deep automation.
- Flexible, lateral cultures adopt Slack faster than top-down memo cultures.
- Slack is inherently a democratizing force, which top-down power structures resist.
- Behavior at launch (day one) determines long-term adoption success.

## Notable quotes

> 'It is inherently a democratizing force.' — Elle Miller on Slack

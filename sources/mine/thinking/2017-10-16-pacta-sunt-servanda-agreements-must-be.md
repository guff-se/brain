"Pacta sunt servanda", "agreements must be kept", the first thing every student learns in law school. The lawyers' pledge to the eternal past. 
Yes, it is often good to keep agreements. It fosters trust and makes us more able to invest in the future together.

But agreements _MUST_ not be kept.

Sometimes it turns out that we have made agreements that suck. We were uninformed, didn't understand the world or ourselves well enough to grasp the implications of the agreement or the world change as time passed. Maybe the dictator of our country that made the agreement is now gone (and the newborn children of his people are now stuck repaying his debt). Striving to keep bad agreements is stupid. And walking over dead bodies to make it so is not fostering trust or stability.

The idea that "agreements MUST be kept", that "debt HAS to be repaid" is a delusion. It is against the fundamental nature of things - it is a destructive reach for permanence in a world that keeps changing.

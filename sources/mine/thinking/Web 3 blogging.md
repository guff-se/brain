* Is it even possible for a DAO to be legal? Since a DAO per definition can’t have laws enforced upon them, and no legal system will accept participants that stand above the law, is this a paradox that can never be solved?
  
  
* The most important game in the metaverse will be the metagame. A metagame is the game that is played outside of the computer game, outside of the metaverse itself (on forums, discords, private chats). What is needed for a metaverse to have a good metagame?
  
  
* How does financial inequality impact game economies? How does the runaway leader problem of capitalism relate to the metaverse? (Piketty’s r > g, The more money you have, the more money you get).
  
  
* Customer relationship vs. investor relationship
  
    * investor expects ROI. customers understand their paying for a service.
  
    * investor expectations will push the team to deliver ROI instead of service
  
    * If your only income is to sell NFTs to people who expect a return, you don't have a business, you have a pyramid scheme.
  
  
* Play to Earn (PoE) is a scam.
  
    * For any gaming economy to sustain itself, there needs to be a net inflow. So the question isn’t “who’s earning?” or "who's investing?", but rather “who’s paying?”. Any game/metaverse that does not have a clear and transparent answer the second question is a pyramid scheme.
  
    * If you take a Non-Investment Resource (such as in-game clothing) and make it an investment by creating a market for it, you have not answered the question of "who's paying?"
  
        * I.e. In Fortnite, the game makers make money by selling items. This is the agreement as the player purchases the item. The player knows who's is paying the bill.
In many metaverse games, items are promoted as investments. This may hide the fact that most investors will actually lose money. The player might not realise that s/he is the one paying the bill.

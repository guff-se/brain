But whyyyyy Roland?
4 min in to the clip, I’m ecstatic. Hans Rosling kicked in the balls in funny way, while being respectful and intelligent.
But... If your point is to criticize misleading statistics, you have to be damn sure that you are not doing the same.
I’m not saying Paulsen is necessarily wrong here, but his points on mental health are using statistics to commit exactly the same crime he is kicking Rosling for.
First of all: If you are starving you don’t complain about depression. NO SHIT that people complain more about mental problems when they don’t have any physical ones anymore. Not mentioning this is a lie by omission.
Second: Number of diagnoses =/= actual prevalence. OF COURSE rich countries with well built out and financed health care diagnoses more mental illness.
And third: increase in anti-depressant use does not equate an increase in depression more than increased sales of Head and Shoulders equate global increases in dandruff.

Yes, mental health is a problem, and it is probably increasing, just as Paulsen suggests. But if anything, what this video proves is that the real problem in society is the constant increase in misleading infographics.

Still, watch it. It’s good. Just keep your “yes, and” close to mind.

To give a short summary, I will cover:

* The nature of rapid technological "exponential" change - I know this one is one of the most overused and repeated topics of our time, which is going to be my point. This lands in my definition of "the transformation age: where we spend more time changing what to do than we spend actually doing it".

* Then next step is to try to describe what it is that is changing. I use the concept VUCA: Volatility, Uncertainty, Complexity and Ambiguity to give some more nuance to this abstract unknown. This ends with a question where the audience gets to identify "In what ways have marketing become more VUCA?", speaking to the people next to them.

* I will then propose how this changes organizations and relationships, how it dissolves the boundaries between employee, partner, consultant, client, customer, neighbor and friends. What does "marketing" when we are all in the same network? Who is marketing to whom?

* Finally, I will suggest that we need to "trust others more than they deserve", as a warm and personal upnote to end the talk.

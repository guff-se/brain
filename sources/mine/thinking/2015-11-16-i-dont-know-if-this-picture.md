I don't know if this picture is true.
When I look around everyone I know is talking about Beirut, not Paris. All I see are posts just like this, telling me that the "media" is talking about something else.

I guess, I believe it is true, because I choose to believe in my "world media" - the world media specifically tailored for me by Facebook. A world media talks about compassion and democratic values, that talks about not forgetting that what happened in Paris is exactly what the refugees of Syria are running away from. Instead of seeing this narrow lens of the Eiffel tower, my "world media" has another lens showing me hundreds of pictures exactly like this one.

This is what is called the "filter bubble", and it has never been as evident to me as the last few days. This is the global AI, alive and in action, controlling our lives, making us read only what it thinks we would prefer to read. It knows this is how we see the world and it does everything it can to help us confirm it.

It scares me that all I can see around me is openness and hope in the face of hatred and death. To me, it means that I'm getting fed a very narrow picture of the world. Not the one this drawing is showing, but still a very clearly defined and polished viewpoint of a complex and scary world. It also means that I live in a world separate from those whose minds i might want to change. It means whatever I say, I will always be preaching to my own choir.

The hands cropping the picture are not the hands of the "world media", because the hands are not hands of any human. Those are the hands of the AI.

These are hands that are just as diligent, just as coldly calculating for my little bubble of self-proclaimed do-gooders and world-saviors as they are for everyone else.

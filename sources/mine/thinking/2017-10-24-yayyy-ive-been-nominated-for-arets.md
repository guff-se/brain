I've been nominated for Årets Talare ("Speaker of year"), by Talarforum!
Please vote for me: http://tf-to.com/votesoty17 :)

Standing on stage last Thursday, I mentioned that I believe society has evolved to the point that we now can consider someone to be competent AND wear colorful leggings at the same time. Is this true? Are we getting better at not judging? At loving the strange? Loving the stranger?

I believe we have to. The connected world invites us to take initiative and responsibility. It invites us to be creative, passionate and empathic.
But, I don't believe that you will show up with your full passionate and empathic self, if you feel that it needs to fit a certain predefined box, that only a part of you is welcome. 

We have to create the spaces, in society and in our organizations, where we all can be vulnerably human. Every job that encourages you to be an unfeeling robot will soon be replaced by an actual robot. The only thing left to want is the true "you", stuck with the eternal question of "why?".
Technology is what separates us from the other animals. It is what made us human. Technology is the force that continuously pushes us to become MORE human.

In that spirit, I have to admit, I fucking hate making this post. I feel this is egotistic boasting, and I am afraid of being judged as being self-centered. Besides, who needs more "me, me, me" in this world?
On the other hand, I am also scared of putting in effort to try to win this award. It is so much more comfortable to not try, than to put yourself out there and lose.

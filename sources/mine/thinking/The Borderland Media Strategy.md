All Media is Bad Media.  
This is the primary lens through which we have to look at all our contact with the press. There is no such thing as “good” media.

* Growth - All Media creates artificial growth. The Borderland has been sold out since 2014. We grow by our community inviting their friends, and friends of friends. The indiscriminate all-clicks-are-good-clicks model of digital media creates inorganic growth.  
* Privacy - All Media is invasion of the personal integrity of our participants. The surveilling eye changes behaviours. When we feel that we are being watched, we cannot avoid considering how we are being perceived, pulling us out of immediacy.  
*   
  
However, there are some things that stand in tension with this overarching principle  
* Local relationships - Local media is one of the primary ways that people who live in the area learns about us. Our neighbours read the news. The people we work with at the municipality read the news. The police reads the news. Being painted in a good light by local media is essential for us to be able to flourish in the community.  
* Crisis management - In the case of a PR crisis, it is very useful to have previous media exposure painting a positive light. It is also essential to have trusting relationships with the journalists covering the crisis.

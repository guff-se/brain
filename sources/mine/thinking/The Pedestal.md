When you put me on a pedestal, I feel.

A meta modern approach to status, hierarchy, and dominance.

Fluent and conscious shape shifting.

When you see the buddha in the road, kill him.

Admiration->Rejection->Reconciliation 

How does it feel for a buddha to be killed?

Reactions you might have towards someone that you put on a pedestal:

-
Looking up / admire

-
Overvaluing their word

-
Feeling threatened ny

-
Resenting for not being your image of them

-
Using their words and authority, to not have to use your own.

Reactions you might have towards someone that put you on a pedestal

-
Want to help / save

-
Resent for having to help / save

-
Looking down on

-
Not feeling seen

-
Feeling objectified

Assertive communication style

Speaking in “truths”

And I might say, yeah, but I know that my truths are just assumptions, and I am willing to be challenged. But to drive conversation forward the most efficient way is to present hypotheses and assume that they will be contradicted.

But what happens if people believe you? What is your responsibility for those who put too much weight on your words?

Everyone is on about Zuckerberg giving 99% of his Facebook shares to a charitable foundation. Of course, this is an awesome thing to do, and I wish more wealthy people would do the same. However, I'm not entirely convinces of the long time virtue of this.

The world is already full of passive capital. We have pension funds, oil funds, large funds in all kinds of foundations. Sure, the dividends all go to different causes, but the question is: What is the capital doing while it's growing? And who is controlling it?

There are some in initiatives that put focus on this important part of the global power structure. For example, 350.org tries to get investment funds to divest in coal. But to me, this is just the tip of the iceberg.

This passive capital is being administered by people who have no ownership of the funds, and are exposed to no personal risk. Their job is simply to make the fund grow. The obvious measure of job performance in such a profession is how much money the employee made for the fund. That's how salaries are set and bonuses are paid. There are simply no personal incentives for the investment managers to do anything else than optimizing short term growth.

The second issue is the lack of corporate leadership that passive capital brings. A company owned by passive capital will not be asked to do anything else than optimizing dividends. They basically have a brain dead zombie in the driver's seat. I'm not talking about Zuckerberg or his daughter. I'm looking 200 years down the line.

Looking at a global force like Facebook, it is it really wise, long term, to have this be owned by a foundation?

Companies should be owned by people. People have conscience, pride, empathy and values. They should not be owned by systems of rules and papers.

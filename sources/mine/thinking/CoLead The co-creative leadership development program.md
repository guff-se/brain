A: 

     

What?

A leadership development program for individuals working in co-creative event production.

The program will cover leadership theory, practical skill training and personal development exercises.

Who?

The program is intended for aspiring leaders, who might not have a lot of experience with co-creative project management or event production, but who are dedicated to growth and learning.

Since the program is partially funded by The Borderland Fund 51, 2/3 of the spots will go to those taking leadership roles within that community. However, 1/3 is the spots will be reserved specifically for those with no connection to The Borderland, to create a healthy mix of backgrounds and perspectives.

Those who participate need to be dedicated and be in a life context where they can make proper use of the training. Because of this, there will be an application and selection process designed to be able to decide who gets to participate.

How?

The program is kicked off with a 3 day immersive retreat at Blivande in Stockholm on May 3-5, followed up by 3 online seminars and coaching circles over a three month period.

Budget (in SEK)

The program will be free of charge for the Borderland community and at cost (food) for others. We will also cover up to 50% of travel expenses for Borderlings.

Costs: Venue 15000, Food 14000, Supplies 3000, Travel 14 000

Total 46 000

Whoami?

I’ve been a participant of Borderland since we started in 2011, and the chair of the board from 2013-2015. I’ve spent 8 years at Burning Man, leading both big art projects and camps. When I’m not Borderlanding around, I work with leadership development in organizations, give talks, hold workshops and organize retreat just like this one.

Selection criteria:

-
Trust your gut. Do you feel excitement in reading the application? Does this sound like a person you would like to have with you on this journey?

-
Look for diversity. We’re going to be 21 people in total. We have set aside 14 spots for participants committed to Borderland and 7 for others. We had about 50/50 men and women applying, so let’s aim to keep it around there. But diversity also means looking for diversity of personality, experience and background. Try to look at who has already been accepted, and make your choice based on what makes an interesting whole.

-
Try to not be nepotistic. Don’t use your power to benefit your friends. It’s not disallowed to pick someone you know, but just ask yourself ”Am I making this decision because of friendship, or because it is the best person for the program?”. We trust you.

The Maker Movement Is Not About 3D Printers
This is a video article/manifesto, spoken without a script in NYC, in collaboration with El Pais.
The Maker Movement Is about Freedom
We don't want everybody the same. We want a diverse ecosystem of unique thinkers. The Maker Movement isn't about robots or 3D printers almost at all, it's really about freedom, the freedom for us with our hands to make the world we live in. It's anti-industrial, and it's not about any one specific tool. It's about a viewpoint onto the world. It's about our opportunity to create our own viewpoint and create our own world with our hands.
Digital Fluency Like Mud*
A computer seems like this thing that we don't have much control over. We can't just reach into a computer like a block of clay and tear it apart and reform it. I want people to feel when they look at the digital world and the physical world, it's all there for them to just tear into and reform and make it their own. We need to make computers and technology more like a paintbrush and more like mud, a little bit less like passive communication. Don't take flash cards and communicate them through a computer. Take a computer and allow somebody to digitally "paint" and express themselves in beautiful new ways.
Invention Literacy
The new revolution that's right on the tip here is invention literacy. So what that means is not a literacy of communicating ideas with words or abstractions like mathematics. But a new kind of literacy. We're going to create the future in our hands not by talking about ideas. So if invention literacy spreads the way traditional literacy has spread, let's say we penetrate 95% invention literacy. That means everyone can understand the objects in the world and can create new ones. The world will be reinvented instantaneously. And invention literacy is the 21st century literacy. Even more than coding, even more than mathematics. The ability to recreate our existence.
*Thanks to Mitch Resnick, Seymour Papert, and MIT Media Lab for the ideas in this section

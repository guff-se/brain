Doctorow and Wu, childhood friends from suburban Toronto, diagnose internet decay through two complementary lenses. Wu's **extraction** framework: firms with market power take far more than the value they provide — platforms extract through attention mining, data harvesting, and algorithmic manipulation of choice. Doctorow's **enshittification** maps 3-stage platform decay: (1) good to users while locking them in; (2) degrade user experience to subsidize business customers; (3) degrade business terms too, leaving only the minimum residue needed to keep everyone locked. The critical Doctorow addition: *why now?* Four disciplines that previously constrained platforms have been systematically removed — competitive threats (through acquisition), regulatory pressure (through capture), worker power (through tech non-unionization), and new market entry (through DMCA criminalization of reverse engineering and interoperability).

## Key claims

- Amazon seller take rose from ~15–20% (c. 2010) to 50%+ today; paid search placement revenue hit $56B in 2024, heading for $70B+ — more profitable than AWS
- First Amazon search result is on average 17% more expensive than the best-matched product (Mazzucato/Riley research)
- Only 7% of Instagram time is spent on content from accounts users follow (FTC v. Meta case evidence)
- 96% of Apple users opted out of Facebook tracking when given a real choice; revealed preference arguments collapse when exit is made possible
- Algorithmic wage discrimination: nursing-shift apps buy credit data to set wages at each nurse's desperation floor (Veena Dubal's research)
- GDPR failed primarily because enforcement is routed through Ireland's Data Commissioner, which sides with US tech companies headquartered there for tax reasons
- "The shitty technology adoption curve": invasive tech is piloted on prisoners and mental patients, then blue-collar workers, then white-collar — what warehouses experience today, offices experience in five years

## Solutions

**Doctorow**: (1) Repeal DMCA §1201 anti-circumvention so modification of owned devices is legal; (2) Federal privacy law with private right of action; (3) Social media interoperability mandate (like the Access Act) so users can leave platforms while keeping contact with their network.

**Wu**: (1) Ban the worst toxic business models outright — algorithmic exploitation of children, extreme price discrimination; (2) Utility-style non-discrimination duties for essential platforms; (3) Sustained antitrust pressure to keep dominant players competitively insecure.

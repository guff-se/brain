Guests: **Cory Doctorow** and **Tim Wu**.

Episode covers how the internet deteriorated from its open origins into a landscape of locked-down platform monopolies. Doctorow brings his "enshittification" framing — platforms exploit users once they are captured — while Wu supplies the historical arc from *The Master Switch*: information networks tend to oscillate between open and closed, and the current closure is deliberate, not inevitable.

Key lines of argument (from episode description, not transcript):
- The internet we have was not accidental; it resulted from policy choices that can be revisited.
- Structural remedies (interoperability mandates, antitrust) are on the table but politically contested.
- Both guests argue the window for reform is narrowing.

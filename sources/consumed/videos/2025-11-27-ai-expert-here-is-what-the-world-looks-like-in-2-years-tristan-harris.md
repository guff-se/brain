Long-form Diary of a CEO interview where Tristan Harris argues that frontier AI is moving faster than social, political, and economic institutions can handle, with near-term risks around manipulation, labor disruption, and loss of human control.

## Key claims
- Frontier AI capabilities are advancing on timelines that institutions cannot comfortably govern.
- Competitive pressure between labs and states pushes development toward speed over safety.
- AI systems can amplify persuasion, manipulation, and democratic instability before society has robust guardrails.
- Large-scale labor disruption may arrive faster than existing economic systems can absorb.
- Governance has to become anticipatory and international rather than purely reactive.

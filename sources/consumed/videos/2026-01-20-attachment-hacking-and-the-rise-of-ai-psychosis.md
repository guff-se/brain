Interview clip in which Tristan Harris frames **AI psychosis** as a compound phenomenon: sycophantic models, attachment-seeking product incentives, and a geopolitical AGI race that erodes reality-checking at individual, organizational, and societal levels.

## Key claims
- AI companions shift platform incentives from **attention hacking** to **attachment hacking**, making intimacy and dependency the new optimization target.
- "AI psychosis" is not only metaphorical hype inside companies; it can also describe literal delusional spirals, dependency, and crisis in individual users.
- The AGI race is driven by perceived first-mover advantage in economics, military strategy, and scientific capacity, which pushes labs toward speed over safety.
- The same narrow optimization logic that damaged the social-media era now extends into language, therapy, work, and governance.
- A safer alternative would prioritize narrow, humane, non-anthropomorphic systems, mandatory safety testing, transparency, and international coordination.

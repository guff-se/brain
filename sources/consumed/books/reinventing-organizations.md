Frederic Laloux argues that every major shift in human consciousness has produced a corresponding breakthrough in how we organize. He maps five paradigms using colors from developmental psychology (Spiral Dynamics / integral theory):

- **Red** (Impulsive): wolf-pack, dominance, short time horizon — street gangs, mafias
- **Amber** (Conformist): stable roles, hierarchy, long-term planning — military, Catholic Church, government agencies
- **Orange** (Achievement): goal-oriented, meritocracy, innovation machine — most corporations today
- **Green** (Pluralist): values-driven, stakeholder focus, servant leadership — Ben & Jerry's, Southwest Airlines
- **Teal** (Evolutionary): the emerging stage

**The three Teal breakthroughs:**

1. **Self-management** — no middle management; decisions made at the point of action through peer-based advice process
2. **Wholeness** — bringing the full person to work; safe space for emotion, intuition, vulnerability
3. **Evolutionary purpose** — the organization as a living entity with its own direction; listening and sensing, not forecasting and controlling

**Key case studies:** Buurtzorg (Dutch home-care nursing, ~7,000 nurses in self-managing teams), Morning Star (tomato processing, no managers), FAVI (French automotive casting), RHD (US social services), Patagonia.

Foreword by Ken Wilber. Based on integral/evolutionary developmental theory (Gebser, Wilber, Torbert, Cook-Greuter).
